Putting the Flash in Flash Mob

Mobflare is a mobile application which allows any number of phone cameras to be synchronized, coordinating them so that they all flash simultaneously or in patterns. You can use it at concerts, parties, sporting events, demonstrations, boring business meetings, or anywhere a bunch of people want to lighten things up.

What Is It Good For?

  • Do "The Wave" with your camera instead of having to jump up and down
  • Kick off a surprise party
  • DIY electric light parade
  • Compete to create the biggest flare
  • Remember that scene in Hitchcock's Rear Window where Jimmy Stewart blinds his attacker with a big flash bulb? Well, one puny little phone camera is not gonna do it, but get a big enough crowd together and perhaps you can stop Godzilla
  • Build a crowdsourced light-field camera (once there's support for taking a picture instead of just firing the flash)
  • Any other legal activity you can dream up

What Do I Need?

  • An Android phone (until someone develops an iPhone port)
  • with a flash on the camera
  • and Internet access (no Bluetooth...yet)
  • also a network-synchronized clock (most phones are set up for this by default; if not, use an app such as ClockSync)
  • plus some friends or neighbors with the same!

What Patterns Are Supported?

  • Single simultaneous flash
  • Repeat simultaneous flash
  • Ripple once through all cameras
  • Ripple repeatedly through all cameras
